Cut Your AI Compute Costs: 5 Quick Wins for Developers

Cost Tips February 2025 4 min read

GPU compute is one of the biggest expenses for AI developers. The good news: with a few smart techniques, you can often cut costs by 30–60% without sacrificing model quality. Here are five practical wins.

1. Use QLoRA Instead of Full Fine-Tuning

QLoRA (Quantized Low-Rank Adaptation) lets you fine-tune large models using a fraction of the VRAM required for full fine-tuning. A 70B model that would require 8× A100s for full fine-tuning can often be QLoRA fine-tuned on a single A100 80GB.

  • Use bitsandbytes for 4-bit quantization.
  • Combine with PEFT library for LoRA adapters.
  • Typical VRAM reduction: 60–75% vs full fine-tuning.

2. Enable Gradient Checkpointing

Gradient checkpointing trades compute for memory by recomputing activations during the backward pass instead of storing them. This reduces peak VRAM usage by up to 60%, allowing larger batch sizes or larger models on the same GPU.

model.gradient_checkpointing_enable()

3. Choose the Right GPU Tier for Your Workload

Not every job needs an H100. Match GPU tier to workload:

  • RTX 4090: Inference, small model testing, ≤7B fine-tuning with QLoRA.
  • A100 40GB: Mid-size models (13B–34B) with QLoRA.
  • A100 80GB: Large models (70B) with QLoRA, or full fine-tuning of smaller models.
  • H100: Only when you need maximum throughput or training 70B+ at full precision.

4. Use Mixed Precision Training (bf16/fp16)

Training in bf16 or fp16 instead of fp32 cuts memory usage roughly in half and speeds up computation on modern GPUs. Most modern training frameworks support this out of the box.

training_args = TrainingArguments(bf16=True, ...)

5. Optimize Your Billing Tier

If you're running long jobs, monthly billing is significantly cheaper than hourly rates. Plan your experiments in batches and switch to monthly plans for sustained workloads. Also:

  • Run experiments during off-peak hours when possible.
  • Use spot/preemptible instances for fault-tolerant training jobs.
  • Ask about student or bulk discounts — many providers offer them.

Quick win summary: QLoRA + gradient checkpointing + bf16 + right GPU tier = typically 40–60% cost reduction with minimal quality loss.
