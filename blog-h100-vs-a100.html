H100 vs A100: Which GPU Should You Choose for LLM Fine-Tuning?

GPU Guide March 2025 5 min read

Choosing between the NVIDIA H100 and A100 is one of the most common questions we get from AI developers. Both are excellent GPUs, but they serve different use cases and budgets. This guide gives you a practical breakdown.

Quick Specs Comparison

Spec A100 (80GB) H100 (80GB)
Memory 80 GB HBM2e 80 GB HBM3
Memory Bandwidth 2 TB/s 3.35 TB/s
FP16 Performance 312 TFLOPS 989 TFLOPS
NVLink Bandwidth 600 GB/s 900 GB/s
Transformer Engine Gen 1 Gen 2 (FP8)

When to Choose the A100

The A100 remains an excellent choice for most fine-tuning workloads. Here's when it makes sense:

  • Budget-conscious projects: A100 rental is significantly cheaper than H100. For many fine-tuning jobs, the cost savings outweigh the performance difference.
  • Models up to 70B parameters: For LoRA/QLoRA fine-tuning of 7B–70B models, the A100 80GB provides sufficient memory and throughput.
  • Inference serving: For serving models in production, the A100 offers excellent price-performance.
  • Multi-GPU training on a budget: Running 4× A100s is often cheaper than 2× H100s with comparable throughput for many workloads.

When to Choose the H100

The H100 is worth the premium in specific scenarios:

  • Large-scale pre-training: If you're training from scratch or doing full fine-tuning on 70B+ models, the H100's FP8 Transformer Engine provides a significant speedup.
  • Time-critical experiments: The H100 is roughly 2–3× faster than the A100 for transformer workloads. If iteration speed matters, the premium pays off.
  • RLHF and complex pipelines: Reinforcement learning from human feedback benefits from the H100's higher memory bandwidth and NVLink throughput.
  • Enterprise SLA requirements: H100 clusters with NVLink provide the highest reliability for production training pipelines.

Practical Recommendation

For most developers and researchers: Start with the A100 80GB. It handles the vast majority of fine-tuning tasks at a lower cost. Upgrade to H100 only when you hit throughput bottlenecks or need to train models larger than 70B parameters.

Bottom Line

The H100 is faster, but the A100 is more cost-efficient for typical fine-tuning workloads. Choose based on your budget and timeline, not just raw specs.
